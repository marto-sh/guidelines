# Domain Vision

This document outlines the high-level vision for the domain, its strategic goals, and the problems it aims to solve. It serves as a guiding star for all development efforts within this domain.

## 1. Executive Summary

A concise overview of the domain's purpose, its primary value proposition, and how it contributes to the overall business strategy.

## 2. Strategic Goals

What are the overarching, long-term objectives for this domain? These should be measurable and aligned with business outcomes.

*   Example: Improve customer retention by 15% within the next year.
*   Example: Reduce operational costs associated with X by 10%.

## 3. Problem Statement

Clearly articulate the core problems this domain is designed to address. Focus on the "why" behind the solutions.

*   What are the current pain points for users or the business?
*   What inefficiencies exist that this domain seeks to resolve?
*   What opportunities are currently unmet?

## 4. Scope and Boundaries

Define what is *in scope* and *out of scope* for this domain. This helps prevent scope creep and clarifies focus.

*   **In Scope**:
    *   List key functionalities and responsibilities.
    *   Identify primary users and their interactions.
*   **Out of Scope**:
    *   Mention areas that will *not* be addressed by this domain.
    *   Clarify dependencies on other domains or external systems.

## 5. Key Stakeholders

Identify the main individuals or groups who have a vested interest in this domain's success.

*   Business Owners
*   Product Managers
*   End Users
*   Operations Team
*   Other Dependent Teams

## 6. High-Level Capabilities

Describe the major features and functions that this domain will provide. These are typically user-centric and solution-agnostic.

*   User Authentication
*   Order Management
*   Payment Processing
*   Content Delivery

## 7. Non-Functional Requirements (High-Level)

Summarize the critical quality attributes that the system must possess.

*   **Performance**: e.g., Response time, throughput.
*   **Scalability**: e.g., Ability to handle increased load.
*   **Security**: e.g., Data protection, access control.
*   **Reliability**: e.g., Uptime, error tolerance.
*   **Maintainability**: e.g., Ease of changes, code quality.

## 8. Guiding Principles

Establish a set of principles that will inform design and implementation decisions within this domain.

*   Example: Prioritize user privacy.
*   Example: Favor simplicity and clarity.
*   Example: Design for resilience.

## 9. Future Vision

A brief look ahead at the potential evolution of the domain. What might it become in 2-5 years?
